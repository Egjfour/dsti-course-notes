# Summary
*The Agile project management framework is defined by short iterations where a determined set of work will be completed. It is popular in software development because of its ease of handling changing requirements. Agile implements scrum processes which are the meetings and tools used.*

# Key Takeaways
1. The Agile method is based on incremental small iterations
2. Automation of processes (e.g., [[Deployment (CI-CD and Containers)|CI/CD]]) is essential in Agile to making sure the team can execute efficiently

# Definitions
- Agile Method: Iterative, incremental method of managing the design and build activities of engineering, information technology and other business areas that aim to provide new product or service development in a highly flexible and interactive manner
- Sprint: A set period of time in which a scrum team commits to accomplishing a certain amount of work

# Notes
## The Agile Manifesto
- Developed in 2001 for software engineering based on four principles
	- Individuals and interaction over processes and tools
	- Working software over comprehensive documentation
	- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
	- <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Responding to change over following a plan</mark>
- Agile uses resources and time to directly create value unlike [[Waterfall|waterfall]] which is more focused on creating requirements
	 ![[Pasted image 20260321140830.png]]
- Keys to success in Agile
	- An involved and committed Product Owner
	- A willing and trained team
	- A collocated team (not a necessity but extremely helpful)
	- A trained and experienced Scrum Master
	- A conductive organizational environment
	- Company culture and contractual environment
	- Automated technical processes
## Scrum Framework
- Roles (Who)
	- Team: Responsible for committing to an iteration and defining the tasks within
		- <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Lives beyond individual projects</mark>
	- Scrum Master: Responsible for adherence to scrum practices and for protecting the team from external interferences
		- Also leads the various meetings
	- Product Owner: Responsible for the broad vision
- Artifacts (What)
	- Product backlog
		- Prioritized list of desired product functionality
		- Functionality <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">must demonstrate business value</mark>
	- Sprint backlog
		- The forecast from the development team about which functionality will be completed in the next iteration
	- Potentially shippable product
		- Based on the definition of "done" which is defined by all relevant scrum roles
	- Burn-down chart
		- A method to monitor (alongside the task board) the tasks completed in a given iteration
- Practices (How)
	- Product planning: 1 hour/week
		- Prioritization, clarification, and estimation (often planning poker)
		- End Result: Product backlog
	- Sprint planning: 2-4 hours/sprint
		- PO ensures cards are ready for sprint planning
		- Dev team decides on capacity and clarifies requirements of tasks
		- End Result: Sprint backlog
	- Daily standup (scrum): 15 minutes/day
		- 3 Questions
			- What did you do yesterday?
			- What will you do today?
			- Any blockers?
		- Also used to update the task board
	- Sprint review: 1-2 hours/sprint
		- Overview of planned vs completed work
		- Demo of work completed
		- Feedback from stakeholders
		- Overall progress report from PO
	- Sprint retrospective 45-90 minutes/sprint
		- Decide on improvements to way the team works
