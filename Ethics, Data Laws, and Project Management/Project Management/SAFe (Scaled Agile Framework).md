# Summary
*As companies continue to grow, agile development becomes increasingly complex with cross-team management playing a central role. The SAFe framework is the adaptation of agile for these large enterprises where many individual agile teams are brought together under a shared purpose. The framework ultimately looks to stack many agile teams together into an agile release train (ART) and organize the work of the ART into programs.*

# Key Takeaways
1. When dealing with large organizations, multiple [[Agile|scrum teams]] must be deployed which introduces additional management complexity

# Definitions
- SAFe: The Scaled Agile Framework is a set of organization and workflow patterns intended to guide enterprises in scaling lean and [[Agile|agile]] practices
- Agile Release Train (ART): Long-lived team of agile teams which incrementally develops, delivers, and operates one or more solutions/products
- Program Backlog: A common business and technology mission that aligns the ART teams

# Notes
## SAFe - An Extension of Agile Principles
- The agile release train is the extension of the agile team
	- Typically, made up of 5 to 12 individual Agile teams which <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">plan, commit, develop, and deploy together</mark>
- A program increment is a higher level iteration of iterations
	- This is the [[Agile|"sprint"]] carried out by the agile release train
		- But it contains the individual sprints that teams complete within
	- Operates on a fixed schedule with <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">all teams synchronized</mark>
	- Typically 4 iterations of 2 weeks + 1 innovation and planning (IP) iteration of 2 weeks
		- The IP acts as an estimating buffer for meeting program increment objectives
		- The IP also is dedicated time for innovation and continuing education
- Program increment planning is a cadence-based face-to-face event that aligns the teams to a shared mission and vision
	- Facilitated by the release train engineer (RTE)
	- Occurs within the IP iteration and over the course of 2 days
	- Two outputs
		- Committed program increment objectives
		- The program board which outlines new feature delivery dates, dependencies among teams, and relevant milestones
- The roles in SAFe are similar to agile with some notable differences
	- The **product manager** is similar to the individual product owners but operates at a higher level supporting the customer needs of the product-market lifecycle
	- The **RTE** is similar to the scrum master but is managing the entire ART and facilitating the ART events
	- The **system architect/engineer** defines and communicates the shared technical and architectural vision for the ART to make sure the solution is fit for purpose
