# Summary
*The waterfall project management methodology is the process of defining upfront all project requirements and deliverables to generate a project charter. This charter represents the full execution plan complete with the necessary milestones and sign-offs. It tends to be the methodology most frequently used by large functional organizations.*

# Key Takeaways
1. The triple constraints triangle illustrates the balance between scope, cost, and time
2. The defining feature of the waterfall method is the upfront planning of timeline and milestones with lots of required signoff

# Definitions
- Project Management: Application of knowledge, skills, tools and techniques to project activities to meet the project requirements
- Waterfall Method: Sequential design process, originating from the manufacturing and construction industries, used in software development processes, in which progress is seen as flowing steadily downwards (like a waterfall) through the phases of conception, initiation, analysis, design, construction, testing, production/implementation and maintenance
- Stakeholder: Any individual or entity who is in any way interested in, affected by, or concerned with the project

## RACI
- The RACI is an acronym that determines what role different stakeholders have on a specific task
	- Responsible
		- The persons who actually carry out the task
	- Accountable
		- The person(s) who are ultimately accountable for the task being completed appropriately
		- Responsible parties report to them
	- Consulted
		- People who do not directly complete the task but are consulted for its execution
		- Subject Matter Experts
	- Informed
		- Those who receive output from the task or have a need to otherwise stay informed
